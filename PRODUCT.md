# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Founders and small teams running direct-to-consumer e-commerce brands (Shopify-style stores, marketplace sellers) who need professional-looking product visuals but can't justify a studio shoot for every drop. They are non-designers, cost- and time-constrained, and juggling this alongside everything else running the business. They arrive comparing VISUAILS against three alternatives: hiring a photographer, using flat marketplace-style product shots, or shooting it themselves on a phone. They typically need visuals for a whole catalog or campaign at once, not a single hero image, so consistency across many SKUs matters as much as any individual photo.

## Product Purpose

VISUAILS turns a single existing product photo into campaign-ready catalog, lifestyle, and video visuals, delivered in about 24 hours and checked by a human before delivery. It exists to remove the cost, time, and logistics of a produced photoshoot for brands that need to look premium at the pace e-commerce actually moves. Success is a founder who orders once on a whim, is surprised by the quality and turnaround, and comes back for their next drop instead of booking a shoot.

## Positioning

One product photo in, a full campaign of visuals out — the consistency and polish of an in-house studio, without booking one.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary and secondary CTA: Primary is a direct order (Order now / Order lifestyle visuals). Secondary, for visitors not ready to commit, is a free test sample made from their own product photo.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: "Send one photo, get a campaign back in about a day."
- Belief ladder: (1) my current product photos aren't good enough to compete → (2) a real photographer or in-house studio costs more time and money than this is worth → (3) VISUAILS can produce results as good as a produced shoot from the photo I already have → (4) it's fast (~24h) and low-risk to find out (free sample, human-reviewed, no card required) → (5) I should try the free sample, then order for the rest of my catalog.
- Proof on hand: none supplied yet — no testimonials, case studies, or client logos are loaded. This is an open item; the site should be built so proof slots (quotes, logos, before/afters) drop in cleanly once available, rather than leaving the belief ladder's weakest rung permanently empty.

## Brand Personality

Assured, warm-dark, precise — quiet luxury applied to a fast, practical service, not a slow one. The voice states the ~24h turnaround and human review as confident proof points, never as apologetic disclaimers. It sells the *feeling* of a produced campaign (stillness, controlled light, deliberate framing) while staying completely legible about the mechanics (one photo in, delivery in about a day, from €19).

## Anti-references

Not a cheap "AI photo generator" gimmick — avoid neon/glitch AI-tool aesthetics, gradient-clip text, or the SaaS hero-metric-and-stats-strip template. Not a busy stock-photo marketplace grid. Not sterile corporate-minimal (all-white, cool-gray startup look) — the brand lives in a warm near-black, not a cold light theme.

## Design Principles

- One photo in, a campaign out: every page should make that trade legible within a few seconds, before any copy is read closely.
- Show, don't tell: the product is visuals, so imagery — styled scenes, real before/afters, real photography — carries more weight than icons, stats, or copy.
- Fast should still read premium: the ~24h turnaround and hand-review are stated with confidence, not hedged like a disclaimer.
- The free sample is the door everyone can walk through: keep it visible everywhere without ever letting it outrank the paid CTA visually.
- Consistency across a whole collection, not one hero shot, is the real differentiator versus one-off AI generators — say this plainly wherever models or style-locking come up.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. `prefers-reduced-motion` is honored throughout (every animation has a static/instant fallback, already the established pattern). Text contrast is verified against the near-black background, not assumed — the muted-ink tokens in DESIGN.md were re-checked and one (`--ink-faint`) was adjusted to clear 4.5:1 for normal-size text.
